Cailean Finn is a new media artist and creative technologist from Waterford, Ireland. His practice investigates computational design and processes embedded within socio-technological systems.
Cailean holds a BSc in Computer Science (2019) from the University of Limerick (UL), and an MA in Art and Technology (2022) from UL.
In his work, Cailean explores emerging technologies, and digital tools to search for new modalities of collaboration with machines; reflecting on the human-machine relationship, and how it could be (re)defined moving forward. He is particularly interested in fields such as artificial intelligence, creative coding, web development and interactive media. Cailean has worked with artists and arts practitioners such as Simone C Niquill, Leon Butler, Peter Power, Yang Ah Ham, Nora √ì Murch√∫ & Soyun Park.
Currently, he is investigating emergent behaviors of virtual life/intelligent agents in the field of evolutionary computation and reinforcement learning.
